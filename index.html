What is Ceviche?

Ceviche is a coastal delicacy originating from Latin America, most notably Peru. It features fresh raw fish cured in citrus juices, like lemon or lime, and spiced with chili peppers.

Essential Ingredients

  • Fresh Sea Bass or Snapper
  • Freshly squeezed lime juice
  • Red onions (thinly sliced)
  • Aji Amarillo or Habanero peppers
  • Fresh cilantro

How to Make Ceviche

  1. Cut fresh fish into small cubes
  2. Place the fish in a bowl
  3. Cover the fish with fresh lemon juice
  4. Let rest for 15-20 minutes to "cure"
  5. Add red onion, tomato and cilantro
  6. Season with salt, pepper and chili peppers
  7. Mix all ingredients well
  8. Serve cold and enjoy with crackers

Ceviche Variations Around the World

Peruvian Ceviche: The classic version with fresh fish, lime juice, red onions, cilantro, and ají peppers. Served with sweet potatoes and corn.

Chilean Ceviche: Uses raw fish marinated in lime juice with onions, cilantro, and fish stock. Often includes avocado for a creamier texture.

Mexican Ceviche: More spicy version with fresh fish, lime juice, jalapeños, tomatoes, and avocado. Sometimes served in tostadas or with chips.
